## How to help your child --



Starting preschool is a big milestone—for both you and your child. It's a mix of excitement, questions, and sometimes a few tears (from both sides!). At *Little Graduates*, we believe this transition can be smooth and even joyful when you know what to expect and how to prepare.



Here’s how you can get started—emotionally and practically.




---




### 👶 **Emotional Prep: Helping Your Child Feel Safe**



1. **Talk about school like an adventure**  
   Keep it positive and familiar. Say things like, *“You’ll have toys to play with, friends to meet, and teachers who will care for you.”*
2. **Practice short separations**  
   Leave them with a relative or sitter for short periods. It helps them get used to the idea that you always come back.
3. **Read preschool-themed books together**  
   Books like *“Llama Llama Misses Mama”* or *“Maisy Goes to Preschool”* can help them visualize and normalize the experience.
4. **Let them express their feelings**  
   It’s okay if they say they’re scared or unsure. Reassure them and keep the conversation open.




---




### 🧦 **Practical Prep: Building Readiness at Home**



1. **Set a routine similar to school timings**  
   Start waking up, eating, and napping around the same time as the school schedule.
2. **Work on basic self-help skills**  
   Encourage them to wash hands, wear shoes, eat with a spoon, or carry their small bag.
3. **Label their belongings**  
   Bag, water bottle, clothes—everything should be easy for the teacher and your child to identify.
4. **Visit the school together**  
   If possible, walk them through the space. Familiarity brings comfort.




---




### ✅ **Checklist for Parents**



* Talk about preschool regularly
* Read books on preschool theme
* Visit the school beforehand
* Practice goodbye routines
* Label all belongings
* Pack comfort items (like a favorite soft toy)
* Start school-day routines a week early
* Make space for big emotions—be patient
* Trust the process (children adapt faster than we think!)
