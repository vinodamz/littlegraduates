# Let's do it Together!! :)



*Inspired by Gavin McCormack’s “The Potential in Every Child”*



At **Kochi's Best Playschool - The Little Graduates Early Learning, Kaloor, Kochi**, we see children blossom when home habits echo what happens in our mixed‑age Montessori classrooms. Below are five bite‑size practices you can start tonight—no special equipment needed, just curiosity and trust.



## 1. Put Them in Charge of Their Things



A two‑year‑old can place blocks back in a basket; a five‑year‑old can wipe a spill. When children steward their space, they build self‑discipline and pride in workmanship. Try a low shelf and labelled baskets to make tidying effortless.



![](/media/2025_06_20250612_112006-577x1024.jpg)



## 2. Repair—Don’t Replace



Next time something breaks, sit together and fix it—glue the mug handle or stitch the teddy’s ear. Children learn mistakes are fixable and grow resilience.


![](/media/2025_06_20250609_170216-768x1024.jpg)



## 3. Take a Weekly Moon Walk



Choose one phone‑free evening stroll. Gaze at the sky and ask, “How do you think the moon stays up there?” Big questions spark big thinking.



![](/media/2025_06_ChatGPT-Image-Jun-12-2025-12_42_05-PM.png)



## 4. Morning Autonomy Ritual



Offer two clothing choices on a low rail every night. Let your child dress themselves and help you leave the house on time—hello, executive‑function boost!



![](/media/2025_06_ChatGPT-Image-Jun-12-2025-12_46_12-PM.png)



## 5. Star‑Lit Storytime



Swap screens for a balcony blanket. Read together, trace constellations, and day‑dream aloud. Shared wonder bonds families and feeds imagination.



![](/media/2025_06_20250610_121244-768x1024.jpg)

### Why It Works



These micro‑habits mirror Montessori principles of independence, hands‑on learning, and cosmic curiosity. Reinforcing them at home accelerates your child’s progress in class.
